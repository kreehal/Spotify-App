Spotify Data and Mental State

Introduction:
In a world where personal data is becoming less and less private, we wanted to investigate the extent to which users' individual information was used by the private sector. Our website allows users to enter their own Spotify listening data, and receive a visualization of the insights that Spotify has the ability to collect. Our program is designed only to educate consumers, and does not store users' individual data.

Our Product Journey:
We took each user’s recently played music, and separated it into distinct listening sessions, which we deemed to be any listening activity that had more than 10 songs, with sessions being separated by any breaks of 30 minutes or more. Once split into listening sessions, we assigned each song an emotional group. The assignment was based on an analysis of the song’s audio features in comparison with data we had previously obtained for that emotional group. From there, we were able to calculate summary statistics for each listening session, based on each song’s emotional group.
Finally, we analyzed the summary statistics of each session in the context of prior research done on the relationship between mood and music choice. Basing our work off of previous findings, we developed a model that would predict user mood for each listening session, which we displayed alongside the summary statistics for user benefit. If you are interested in learning more about our research, data collection and analysis, or emotional analysis framework, you can find a detailed slideshow describing our work here.
